Maderna Machine — Magic Squares

Bruno Maderna's shifting technique (1951), reconstructed

Type one twelve-tone row and see it as a square and as rhythmless note-heads; then unfold the full machine — magic square + shifting factors → derived squares → 108 sets → rhythmic realization — with MusicXML downloads at every stage. After Veniero Rizzardi's study of the serial machine behind Improvvisazione n. 1.

The technique. Maderna turns a single twelve-tone row into a large field of derived material by graphic manipulation on squared paper. The row is drawn as a 12×12 square (pitches × time-positions, one dot per column). A separate magic square — a Latin square of 12 symbols — maps, via a key, to integer shifting factors; applied note-by-note, each factor says how far to the right the next dot is drawn, advancing across a strip of successive charts. The derived squares contain repeated dots, vertical aggregates (Maderna's "harmonic projection") and empty columns (rests): the row has mutated (mutazione) from a theme into a generative distribution. Nine "proportions" (A–I) yield 9 × 12 = 108 sets, ordered by a classification chart — the "structural key of the whole piece" — and realized rhythmically through three dance patterns (waltz, polka, cancan) in an isorhythmic-motet logic.

Honesty note. The exact arithmetic of wrapping, of how empty boxes and aggregates arise, and of the reading order is reconstructed from Rizzardi's description of Maderna's sketches — it is not a fully specified historical algorithm. This app implements a clean, self-consistent, parameterized model that faithfully reproduces the documented behavior (heterogeneous sets of notes, aggregates and rests; 108 squares across 9 proportions; the mutation of the row; a classification chart) and exposes the parameters so you can experiment. It is a computational reconstruction, not a box-for-box reproduction of the sketches. Note also that Maderna's magic square and shift-factor key were themselves compositional choices, independent of the row — the app defaults them so a single row runs the whole machine, but exposes them because the apparatus, not the row alone, is what generates the music.

Model choices in this reconstruction. Each proportion takes 12 derivation steps; step j of proportion P reads row (j+P) mod 12 of the magic square (even proportions) or the corresponding column (odd proportions), so each proportion reads the apparatus differently. Every pitch collects each factor exactly once per cycle, advancing by the full factor sum — hence the pattern returns to its start and seeds the next proportion. The trail of dots is cut back into 12×12 charts; early dots may re-enter the first chart (a documented "repeated dot" phenomenon).

Pitch coding (Maderna's, chromatic from A):
1=A · 2=B♭ · 3=B · 4=C · 5=C♯ · 6=D · 7=E♭ · 8=E · 9=F · 10=F♯ · 11=G · 12=A♭
